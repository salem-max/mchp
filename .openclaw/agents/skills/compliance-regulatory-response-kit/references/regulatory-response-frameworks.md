# Regulatory Response Frameworks for CRE

## Framework 1: Building Code Violations

### Violation Classification

| Severity | Description | Response Window | Examples |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Imminent hazard** | Life-safety threat requiring immediate action | 24-72 hours | Structural failure, no fire egress, electrical hazard |
| **Major violation** | Code non-compliance affecting occupant safety | 30-90 days | Non-functional fire alarm, deficient sprinkler, ADA barrier |
| **Minor violation** | Technical or administrative non-compliance | 90-180 days | Missing permit, signage deficiency, record-keeping gap |
| **Notice of violation** | Observed condition requiring correction | Per NOV terms | Maintenance issue, cosmetic deficiency |

### Inspection Preparation Protocol

**Pre-inspection checklist** (complete before any scheduled or anticipated inspection):

1. **Documentation ready**:
   - [ ] Certificate of Occupancy (current, matching actual use)
   - [ ] Building permits (all open permits closed out or active)
   - [ ] Fire alarm monitoring certificate (current)
   - [ ] Elevator inspection certificate (current, posted in cab)
   - [ ] Sprinkler system inspection report (annual, current)
   - [ ] Boiler inspection certificate (if applicable)
   - [ ] Backflow preventer test report (annual)
   - [ ] Emergency action plan (posted and current)
   - [ ] Maintenance logs (HVAC, electrical, plumbing -- 3 years minimum)

2. **Physical preparation**:
   - [ ] All egress paths clear and unobstructed
   - [ ] Exit signs illuminated and visible
   - [ ] Fire extinguishers in place, tagged, current inspection
   - [ ] Emergency lighting functional
   - [ ] Fire doors closing and latching properly
   - [ ] Electrical panels accessible (36-inch clearance, labeled)
   - [ ] Mechanical rooms clean and accessible
   - [ ] Roof access secured but available
   - [ ] Stairwell doors operating correctly (proper hardware, no propping)

3. **Staff briefing**:
   - Assign one person to escort inspector (management level)
   - Escort takes notes on every area inspected and every comment made
   - Do not volunteer information beyond the scope of inspection
   - If inspector asks a question you cannot answer: "I will need to verify that and follow up"
   - Do not argue with inspector on-site. Note disagreements for counsel.
   - Request a copy of any written violation before inspector leaves

### Re-Inspection Protocol

1. Confirm all remediation work is complete and inspectable
2. Gather all documentation: permits, contractor certifications, test reports
3. Request re-inspection through the jurisdiction's scheduling system
4. Have contractor on-site or on-call during re-inspection
5. If re-inspection fails: document deficiency, correct immediately, re-request
6. Obtain written clearance / violation closure documentation
7. File in permanent property compliance record

## Framework 2: OSHA Citations

### Citation Types and Penalties (2026 rates, adjusted annually)

| Type | Description | Per-Violation Penalty | Per-Day Failure to Abate |
|------|-------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| **De minimis** | Technical violation, no direct safety impact | None (notice only) | N/A |
| **Other-than-serious** | Violation with direct relationship to safety but unlikely to cause death/serious harm | Up to $16,500 | Up to $16,500 |
| **Serious** | Substantial probability of death or serious harm, employer knew or should have known | $1,190 - $16,500 | Up to $16,500 |
| **Willful** | Intentional disregard or plain indifference | $11,524 - $165,514 | Up to $16,500 |
| **Repeat** | Same or similar violation within 5 years | Up to $165,514 | Up to $16,500 |

### Contest vs. Settle Decision Tree

```
Citation received
    |
    v
Is the citation factually accurate?
    |
    +-- NO --> Contest the citation (15 business days from receipt)
    |           File Notice of Contest with Area Director
    |           Case goes to OSHRC (Occupational Safety & Health Review Commission)
    |
    +-- YES --> Is the classification correct?
                    |
                    +-- NO --> Request informal conference to negotiate reclassification
                    |           (reduce from willful to serious, serious to other-than-serious)
                    |
                    +-- YES --> Is the penalty reasonable?
                                    |
                                    +-- NO --> Request informal conference for penalty reduction
                                    |
                                    +-- YES --> Abate the hazard, pay the penalty
```

### Informal Conference Strategy

Request within 15 business days of citation. The Area Director has authority to:
- Reduce penalties
- Reclassify violations
- Extend abatement dates
- Delete citation items

**Penalty reduction arguments**:

| Factor | Argument | Typical Reduction |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **Size** | Fewer than 250 employees (sliding scale) | 20-60% |
| **Good faith** | Active safety program, voluntary compliance history | 15-25% |
| **History** | No prior citations in 5 years | 10% |
| **Gravity** | Low probability and/or low severity | Adjust base penalty down |
| **Quick fix** | Hazard abated before or during inspection | 10-15% (informal) |
| **Cooperation** | Full cooperation during inspection and conference | 5-10% (informal) |

## Framework 3: ADA Compliance

### Self-Inspection Methodology

ADA Title III requires places of public accommodation to remove barriers where "readily achievable" (easily accomplishable without much difficulty or expense).

**Priority order for barrier removal** (DOJ recommended):

| Priority | Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Access to the facility | Parking, exterior routes, entrance |
| 2 | Access to goods and services | Interior routes, checkout, service counters |
| 3 | Restroom access | Stall size, grab bars, fixtures, signage |
| 4 | Additional access | Drinking fountains, phones, other amenities |

### Self-Inspection Checklist (Key Items)

**Parking**:
- [ ] Correct number of accessible spaces (1 per 25 total, minimum)
- [ ] At least 1 van-accessible space per 6 accessible spaces
- [ ] 60-inch access aisle (96-inch for van-accessible)
- [ ] Surface: firm, stable, slip-resistant, max 2% cross-slope
- [ ] Signage: international symbol, "Van Accessible" where applicable, mounted 60 inches minimum
- [ ] Closest to accessible entrance

**Entrance**:
- [ ] At least one accessible entrance (32-inch clear width minimum)
- [ ] Threshold: max 1/2 inch (1/4 inch for non-beveled)
- [ ] Door hardware: lever or push-type (no round knobs)
- [ ] Door closing speed: minimum 5 seconds from 90 degrees to 12 degrees
- [ ] Automatic door opener or accessible hardware at primary entrance

**Interior Routes**:
- [ ] 36-inch minimum width (44 preferred)
- [ ] No protruding objects above 27 inches from floor (max 4-inch protrusion)
- [ ] Floor surfaces: firm, stable, slip-resistant
- [ ] Changes in level: max 1/4 inch vertical, 1/2 inch with bevel
- [ ] Ramps where needed: max 1:12 slope, handrails both sides, edge protection

**Restrooms**:
- [ ] At least one accessible stall: 60x60 inch minimum floor space
- [ ] Grab bars: 42 inches side wall, 36 inches rear wall, 33-36 inches above floor
- [ ] Toilet: 17-19 inch seat height, centerline 16-18 inches from side wall
- [ ] Lavatory: 34-inch maximum height, knee clearance 27 inches, 30 inches wide, 19 inches deep
- [ ] Mirror: 40-inch maximum reflective surface height (above counter)
- [ ] Signage: raised characters, braille, 60-inch centerline

### Readily Achievable Standard

Factors for determining "readily achievable":
1. Nature and cost of the action
2. Overall financial resources of the facility
3. Overall financial resources of the entity
4. Type of operation

**Rule of thumb**: If barrier removal costs less than 1% of annual gross revenue for the property, it is generally readily achievable. Document all decisions with cost estimates and financial analysis.

### Transition Plan (for new construction or alterations)

When renovations exceed the "path of travel" threshold (20% of total alteration cost), an accessible path of travel to the altered area is required. This includes:
- Accessible route from entrance to altered area
- Accessible restrooms serving the altered area
- Accessible drinking fountains and telephones on the accessible route

## Framework 4: Environmental Compliance During Construction

### SWPPP (Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan)

Required for construction sites disturbing 1+ acres (or part of a common plan of development disturbing 1+ acres).

**Key components**:
1. **Site description**: Maps, drainage patterns, soil types, receiving waters
2. **Erosion and sediment controls**: Silt fence, sediment basins, inlet protection, stabilized construction entrance
3. **Good housekeeping BMPs**: Material storage, waste management, spill prevention
4. **Inspection schedule**: Weekly and within 24 hours of 0.5+ inch rainfall events
5. **Corrective actions**: Document deficiencies and repairs

### Inspection Requirements

| Trigger | Inspection Required | Documentation |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| Weekly | Routine SWPPP inspection | Log with date, inspector name, findings, corrective actions |
| Rain event > 0.25 inch | Within 24 hours of cessation | Photograph all BMPs, note any failures or needed repairs |
| Rain event > 0.5 inch | Within 24 hours of cessation | Full inspection with documented corrective actions |
| Discharge visible | Immediately | Sample if required by permit, implement corrective action |
| Regulatory inspection | Escort inspector | Maintain complete SWPPP binder on-site |

### Dust Control Requirements

| Control Measure | Application | Frequency |
|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| Water truck | Active earthwork areas | As needed to prevent visible emissions |
| Soil stabilizer | Inactive disturbed areas | Upon 14 days of inactivity |
| Gravel tracking pad | Construction entrance | Maintain at all times |
| Wind screen/fence | Stockpiles, perimeter | Permanent during construction |
| Cover stockpiles | When wind > 25 mph or inactive > 7 days | As triggered |
| Street sweeping | Adjacent public roads | Daily when track-out occurs |

### Erosion Prevention Best Practices

1. **Minimize disturbance**: Only clear areas actively under construction
2. **Phase construction**: Stabilize completed areas before opening new disturbance
3. **Protect slopes**: Install slope drains, terracing, or erosion blankets on cuts > 3:1
4. **Perimeter controls**: Silt fence at all downslope perimeters, maintained upright with no gaps
5. **Inlet protection**: All storm drain inlets within or adjacent to site must be protected
6. **Sediment basins**: Required for drainage areas > 10 acres; size for 2-year, 24-hour storm
7. **Stabilization**: Temporary seed and mulch within 14 days of final grade on any area

### Spill Response Protocol

```
IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (within minutes):
1. Stop the source of the spill
2. Protect nearby storm drains (deploy absorbent booms, cover inlets)
3. Contain the spill (berms, absorbent materials)
4. Do NOT flush spill toward storm drains or water bodies

NOTIFICATION (within hours):
1. On-site environmental manager
2. Project manager
3. Property owner representative
4. Regulatory agency (if reportable quantity exceeded)
   - National Response Center: 1-800-424-8802 (for releases of hazardous substances)
   - State environmental agency hotline

CLEANUP:
1. Remove contaminated soil/materials
2. Properly characterize and dispose of waste
3. Document all actions with photographs and chain of custody
4. Amend SWPPP to prevent recurrence
5. Report to regulatory agency if required
```

### Common Penalties

| Violation | Federal Penalty (EPA) | Typical State Penalty |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| No SWPPP | Up to $64,618/day | $5,000 - $25,000/day |
| Inadequate BMPs | Up to $64,618/day | $2,500 - $10,000/day |
| Failure to inspect | Up to $64,618/day | $1,000 - $5,000/day |
| Unpermitted discharge | Up to $64,618/day | $10,000 - $50,000/day |
| Failure to notify | Up to $64,618/day + criminal referral | $5,000 - $25,000 + criminal |

**Note**: Penalties are adjusted annually for inflation. Criminal penalties apply for knowing violations or negligent endangerment.
