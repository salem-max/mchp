# Stakeholder Communication Templates for CRE

## Template 1: Construction Notice to Adjacent Properties

### Pre-Construction Notification Letter

```
[Date]
[Property Owner/Manager Name]
[Address]

RE: Upcoming Construction Activity at [Project Address]

Dear [Name],

We are writing to inform you of upcoming construction activity at [project address],
located [direction/distance] from your property at [their address].

PROJECT OVERVIEW
- Project: [Brief description -- e.g., "Construction of a 150-unit multifamily
  residential building"]
- Developer: [Company name]
- General Contractor: [Company name]
- Expected start date: [Date]
- Expected completion date: [Date]
- Construction hours: [Days and hours -- e.g., "Monday through Friday,
  7:00 AM to 6:00 PM; Saturday 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM; no Sunday or holiday work"]

WHAT TO EXPECT
During construction, you may experience:
- Increased truck traffic on [street names] during delivery hours ([time range])
- Noise from construction equipment, particularly during [specific activities]
- Temporary lane closures or traffic modifications on [street names]
  during [date range] for [utility work, crane operations, etc.]
- [Any other impacts specific to the project]

WHAT WE ARE DOING TO MINIMIZE IMPACT
- All work will comply with the city's noise ordinance
- Truck routes have been designed to minimize residential street usage
- Dust mitigation measures (water trucks, covered haul trucks) will be in place
- A dedicated construction entrance will keep equipment off [street name]
- [Other mitigation measures]

CONTACT INFORMATION
If you have questions, concerns, or experience any issues related to the
construction activity, please contact:

  [Name], Community Relations Manager
  Phone: [Number]
  Email: [Email]
  Available: [Days/hours]

We are committed to being a good neighbor throughout this process. Thank you
for your patience and understanding.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Title]
[Company]
```

## Template 2: Noise and Traffic Mitigation Plan

### Construction Mitigation Summary (for neighborhood distribution)

```
[PROJECT NAME] -- CONSTRUCTION MITIGATION PLAN

NOISE MANAGEMENT
- Construction hours: [hours] -- no work outside permitted hours
- Backup alarm modification: all equipment uses broadband (white noise)
  backup alarms instead of traditional beeping where permitted by OSHA
- Impact equipment (pile driving, jackhammering): limited to [hours]
- Noise monitoring: continuous monitoring at property line with real-time
  alerts if thresholds exceeded
- Maximum permitted noise at property line: [dBA level per local ordinance]

TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT
- Delivery hours: [hours] -- heavy deliveries restricted to off-peak [hours]
- Truck route: [describe approved route, avoiding residential streets]
- Flaggers/traffic control: present at [intersection] during [activities/dates]
- Worker parking: [designated lot/area], not on residential streets
- Estimated daily truck trips: [number], primarily during [hours]

DUST AND DEBRIS CONTROL
- Water trucks: active during all earthwork operations
- Stabilized construction entrance: prevents mud tracking onto public roads
- Street sweeping: [frequency] on adjacent streets
- Covered trucks: all haul trucks covered when leaving site
- Perimeter fencing: 8-foot construction fence with screening

VIBRATION MANAGEMENT
- Pre-construction survey of adjacent structures within [distance]
- Vibration monitoring during [specific activities]
- Threshold: [inches/second peak particle velocity]
- If threshold approached: stop work, evaluate, modify methods

DRAINAGE AND WATER
- Stormwater pollution prevention plan (SWPPP) in place
- Erosion controls maintained throughout construction
- No construction water discharge to public storm drains

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
- Fire department access maintained at all times
- Emergency contact available 24/7: [Name, Number]

SCHEDULE OF SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITIES
| Activity | Estimated Dates | Impact Level | Mitigation |
|----------|----------------|--------------|------------|
| Demolition | [dates] | High (noise, dust) | Water, sound barriers |
| Excavation | [dates] | High (trucks, noise) | Traffic control, water |
| Foundation | [dates] | Moderate (concrete trucks) | Delivery schedule |
| Steel erection | [dates] | Moderate (crane, noise) | Lane closure plan |
| Exterior work | [dates] | Low-moderate | Scaffolding, netting |
| Interior fit-out | [dates] | Low | Minimal exterior impact |
| Landscaping | [dates] | Low | Final restoration |
```

## Template 4: Complaint Response

### Acknowledgment (within 24 hours)

```
Subject: RE: Your Concern Regarding [Issue] -- Case #[Number]

Dear [Name],

Thank you for bringing this to our attention. We have received your
[call/email/report] regarding [brief description of complaint] and have
assigned it Case #[Number] for tracking.

We take all community feedback seriously. Here is what happens next:

1. Investigation: Our team will investigate the reported issue within
   48 hours
2. Response: We will contact you with our findings and any corrective
   actions by [date, within 72 hours]
3. Follow-up: If the issue requires ongoing remediation, we will provide
   regular updates until resolved

Your contact for this matter is [Name] at [phone/email].

Thank you for your patience. We are committed to addressing this promptly.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Title]
```

### Resolution Response (within 72 hours)

```
Subject: Resolution Update -- Case #[Number]: [Issue]

Dear [Name],

Following up on your report of [issue] on [date], we have completed
our investigation. Here is what we found and what we have done:

FINDINGS
[Factual description of what was found -- e.g., "Our investigation confirmed
that construction deliveries on [date] occurred 15 minutes before the
permitted start time of 7:00 AM."]

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
[Specific actions taken -- e.g., "We have reinforced delivery scheduling
protocols with our general contractor and posted the permitted hours at
the site entrance. The site superintendent has been briefed."]

PREVENTION
[What will prevent recurrence -- e.g., "A time-lock has been added to the
site gate to prevent access before 7:00 AM."]

If you experience this issue again or have any other concerns, please
contact us immediately at [phone/email] referencing Case #[Number].

We appreciate your feedback and your patience as we work to be a
responsible neighbor.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Title]
```

## Template 5: Public Hearing Testimony Outline

### Structure for Zoning/Land Use Hearing Testimony

```
PUBLIC HEARING TESTIMONY OUTLINE
Project: [Name]
Hearing Body: [Planning Commission / Zoning Board / City Council]
Hearing Date: [Date]
Application: [Type -- e.g., Special Use Permit, Variance, Rezoning]

I. INTRODUCTION (2 minutes)
   - State name, role, relationship to project
   - "Thank you, [Chair/Members], for the opportunity to present"
   - Brief project description: what, where, why

II. PROJECT MERITS (5 minutes)
   A. Consistency with comprehensive plan
      - Cite specific plan policies the project supports
      - Reference future land use map designation

   B. Community benefits
      - Employment: construction jobs, permanent jobs
      - Tax revenue: estimated annual property tax, sales tax
      - Public improvements: infrastructure, streetscape, parks
      - Housing: units, affordability component (if applicable)

   C. Design and compatibility
      - Scale and massing relative to surroundings
      - Architectural quality and materials
      - Landscaping and screening
      - Traffic study conclusions: LOS maintained at key intersections

III. STANDARDS COMPLIANCE (3 minutes)
   [Address each applicable approval standard specifically]
   - Standard 1: [Quote standard, explain how project meets it]
   - Standard 2: [Quote standard, explain how project meets it]
   - [Continue for all applicable standards]

IV. COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT (2 minutes)
   - Outreach conducted: [number] community meetings, newsletters,
     door-to-door
   - Feedback received and how project was modified in response
   - Ongoing commitments: construction mitigation plan, community
     liaison, complaint hotline

V. CONDITIONS OFFERED (2 minutes)
   - Proactively offer reasonable conditions that address likely concerns
   - "We are prepared to accept conditions including..."
   - [List specific conditions -- hours of operation, traffic mitigation,
     phasing, landscaping, etc.]

VI. CONCLUSION (1 minute)
   - Restate key benefits
   - "We respectfully request approval of [application type]"
   - "We are available to answer any questions"

VISUAL AIDS TO PREPARE:
- [ ] Site plan (large format, visible from dais)
- [ ] Rendering from key viewpoints
- [ ] Context map showing project in neighborhood
- [ ] Traffic study summary (1 page)
- [ ] Community benefit summary (1 page)
- [ ] Letters of support (if any)

ANTICIPATE OPPOSITION ON:
- Traffic impact
- Building height/density
- Construction disruption
- Property values (address with comparable evidence)
- Environmental concerns
- Parking
- [Other project-specific issues]
```

## Template 6: Neighborhood Meeting Agenda

### Community Meeting Agenda Template

```
COMMUNITY MEETING
[Project Name]
[Date] | [Time] | [Location]

AGENDA

6:00 PM    OPEN HOUSE / DISPLAY BOARDS
           - Project plans and renderings on display
           - Team members available for one-on-one questions
           - Comment cards available

6:30 PM    FORMAL PRESENTATION
           Welcome and Introductions .................. [Moderator]
           Project Overview ........................... [Developer]
           Design and Architecture .................... [Architect]
           Traffic and Parking ........................ [Traffic Engineer]
           Construction Timeline and Mitigation ....... [General Contractor]
           Community Benefits ......................... [Developer]

7:00 PM    QUESTION AND ANSWER SESSION
           [Moderated Q&A -- written questions collected,
            each question read aloud and answered]

7:45 PM    NEXT STEPS AND HOW TO STAY INVOLVED
           - Upcoming hearing dates and how to participate
           - Project website and contact information
           - Sign-up for email updates
           - How to submit written comments

8:00 PM    ADJOURN
           [Team available for additional one-on-one conversations]

MEETING GROUND RULES (posted and announced):
1. One speaker at a time
2. Questions through the moderator
3. Please keep questions/comments to 2 minutes
4. All questions and comments will be documented
5. Written comments carry equal weight to verbal comments
6. We are here to listen -- disagreement is welcome,
   personal attacks are not

POST-MEETING FOLLOW-UP:
- [ ] Compile all questions and comments within 48 hours
- [ ] Draft written responses to all questions
- [ ] Post Q&A summary on project website within 1 week
- [ ] Send follow-up email to all attendees with Q&A document
- [ ] Log meeting in community relations file
- [ ] Identify any project modifications warranted by feedback
```
