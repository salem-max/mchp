# Fund Capital Raise -- LP Fee Concession Framework

> conditions with placement agents -- benchmarks reflect mid-2025 institutional

## 1. Concession Tiers by LP Commitment Size

### 1.1 Anchor LP (>15% of Target Fund)

**Definition:** Single LP committing more than 15% of the fund target. On a
$300M fund, anchor threshold is $45M+.

**Standard fee concession range:** 15-30 bps below standard management fee.
- For a 1.50% standard fee: anchor pays 1.20%-1.35%.
- Rarely exceed 30 bps below standard -- beyond this, the GP sets an MFN floor
  that constrains all subsequent negotiations.

**Non-fee concessions included as standard for anchors:**
- Advisory committee (LPAC) seat -- typically guaranteed, not advisory
- Enhanced reporting package: quarterly property-level financials, monthly
  variance reports, annual valuation summary with comparable support
- Co-invest priority: first look at deal-by-deal co-invest opportunities,
  typically fee-free or at 50 bps vs standard 1.00% co-invest fee
- GP availability: direct access to PM/CIO, semi-annual one-on-one calls

**Anchor economics rationale:**
- Anchors reduce GP execution risk for the first close
- An anchor at 30% of target can enable a first close at or above minimum
  viable size, unlocking the rest of the raise
- GPs often accept deeper discounts to secure the first close signal to the
  market -- but must model the MFN cascade before agreeing

**Negotiation leverage points:**
- Anchor LP's decision timeline (closer to their fiscal year-end = more
  urgency for them, less for GP)
- Whether the anchor is bringing relationship LPs (adds raise velocity value)
- Whether the anchor is a public pension with public disclosure obligations
  (side letter terms may become public via FOIA)

---

### 1.2 Strategic LP (5-15% of Target Fund)

**Definition:** LP committing 5%-15% of fund target. On a $300M fund: $15M-$45M.

**Standard fee concession range:** 10-20 bps below standard.
- For a 1.50% standard fee: strategic LP pays 1.30%-1.40%.
- The 10 bps minimum concession is near-universal at this commitment level --
  strategic LPs with institutional programs expect it.

**Non-fee concessions typically offered (pick 1-2, not all):**
- Co-invest allocation: pro-rata share of co-invest, often fee-free up to a
  cap (e.g., up to 25% of commitment in co-invest, fee-free)
- LPAC observer seat (voting seat reserved for anchors)
- Reporting: quarterly reports one business day earlier than standard LPs
- Advisory input: included in annual LP advisory call, occasional strategy
  consultation calls

**Notes for strategic tier:**
- Do not combine all concessions simultaneously -- bundle only what is needed
  to close the commitment
- Strategic LPs near the lower bound (5-7% of target) should receive fewer
  concessions than those near the upper bound (13-15%)
- Watch MFN interaction: if an anchor at 20% gets 1.20% and a strategic at
  8% gets 1.30%, the strategic is above the MFN floor -- safe, no cascade

---

### 1.3 Standard LP (1-5% of Target Fund)

**Definition:** LP committing 1%-5% of fund target. On a $300M fund: $3M-$15M.

**Standard fee concession range:** 0-10 bps below standard.
- 0 bps: no discount, standard terms
- 5-10 bps: only for early closers who sign prior to first close deadline
- Commitment-size fee tiers are the preferred mechanism here (e.g., 1.50%
  below $10M, 1.40% for $10M-$25M) rather than negotiated discounts

**Non-fee concessions that may apply:**
- Early closer discount (5-10 bps) if signing at first close
- Standard co-invest access (1.00% fee, pro-rata allocation with no priority)
- Standard quarterly reporting

**Key GP discipline for standard tier:**
- Resist individual negotiation -- direct LPs to commitment-tier schedule in
  the PPM, not side letter negotiation
- Standard LPs requesting 25+ bps discounts should be redirected to discuss
  whether they can increase commitment to strategic tier instead
- Concessions granted here directly reduce GP economics since the MFN floor
  impact is disproportionate relative to the commitment size

---

### 1.4 Emerging LP (<1% of Target Fund)

**Definition:** LP committing less than 1% of fund target. On a $300M fund:
below $3M.

**Standard fee concession range:** None -- standard terms apply.
- Minimum ticket sizes typically $1M-$5M depending on fund strategy
- No side letter negotiation for emerging-tier LPs (unless relationship-
  mandated)
- In rare cases, an institutional LP with a small initial ticket who has
  signaled follow-on capacity (seed investor, new program) may receive
  emerging-tier treatment as a relationship accommodation

**Risks of granting concessions to emerging LPs:**
- High MFN blast radius relative to commitment: a 25 bps concession on a
  $2M commitment ($5K/yr) could cascade to 30 other LPs
- Precedent-setting: IR teams can lose control of the process if small LPs
  learn that individual negotiation is possible
- Legal cost of side letters not justified by commitment size

## 2. Non-Fee Concessions That Preserve Economics

The most effective GP counter-offer strategy is to offer value that costs
less economically than a fee reduction -- especially when MFN cascade would
multiply the cost of a fee cut.

### 2.1 Fee-Free Co-Invest

**Mechanics:** LP receives allocation rights to co-invest in deals at no
management fee (0%) and reduced or no carry (e.g., 5% instead of 20%).

**Economic value to LP:**
- On a $50M commitment with 15% co-invest right at 1.00% fee waived:
  $7.5M potential co-invest * 1.00% fee waived = $75K/yr value at fund level
- At a 20% IRR, the carry waiver on a $7.5M co-invest over 5 years is
  meaningful (approximately $300K-$500K depending on timing)

**Why GP prefers this:**
- Co-invest is deployed separately from the fund, so no MFN cascade on fund
  management fee
- The fee waiver applies only to the LP's co-invest tranche
- If the co-invest doesn't get deployed (deal not done), the concession has
  zero cost

**Standard allocation:**
- Anchor: unlimited priority co-invest, fee-free, 5-10% carry
- Strategic: 25% of commitment in co-invest, fee-free, standard carry
- Standard: no preferential co-invest

---

### 2.2 Advisory Committee / LPAC Seat

**Value:** Governance access, visibility into fund operations, ability to
vote on GP conflicts of interest.

**What an LPAC seat gives:**
- Review and approval rights on GP conflicts (related-party transactions,
  cross-fund allocations)
- Access to annual fund valuation methodology review
- Ability to call a GP removal vote (in extreme cases under fund documents)

**Cost to GP:**
- Time cost only -- typically two 2-hour calls per year
- Risk: LPAC LPs have more visibility into GP operations and conflicts

**When to offer:**
- As an alternative to a 10+ bps fee reduction for strategic LPs
- When an LP is a major institutional investor whose endorsement matters for
  the next fund

---

### 2.3 Enhanced Reporting

**Standard institutional reporting:** Quarterly financials, annual audit,
annual K-1, semiannual property report.

**Enhanced reporting package options:**
- Monthly property-level income statements with prior-period variance
- Quarterly valuation methodology letter (not just the number)
- Annual fund benchmarking against NCREIF or MSCI REALPAC
- Bi-annual call with asset management team (not just IR)
- Custom data extracts in LP's preferred format (Excel vs PDF)

**Cost to GP:** Minimal marginal cost per LP once reporting infrastructure
is built. High perceived value to institutional LPs with internal compliance
requirements.

---

### 2.4 Sidecar / Co-Invest Rights

**Mechanics:** LP receives right (not obligation) to participate in specific
deals alongside the fund on deal-by-deal terms.

**Types:**
- **Pro-rata sidecar:** LP can invest up to X% of their fund commitment in
  each qualifying deal
- **Discretionary sidecar:** GP offers deals to LP on an ad-hoc basis
- **Sector-specific sidecar:** LP only receives co-invest rights in specific
  property types (e.g., industrial only)

**Structuring considerations:**
- Define the co-invest fee structure in the side letter upfront
- Standard: 0.50%-1.00% management fee, standard carry
- Preferred co-invest (no fee, reduced carry): anchor-only
- Be explicit about whether co-invest rights are transferable (generally not)

---

### 2.5 Opt-Out Rights

**Mechanics:** LP may opt out of specific investments that conflict with
their investment guidelines (e.g., certain geographies, sectors, or debt
levels).

**Common opt-out trigger examples:**
- Property type: insurance companies may need to avoid certain sectors for
  regulatory reasons
- Geography: some state pension plans have political restrictions
- Leverage: endowments with leverage constraints may need to opt out of
  high-LTV deals

**GP considerations:**
- Unlimited opt-out rights make fund management difficult -- cap at 2-3
  opt-outs per investment period
- Opt-out must be exercised within a defined window (typically 10-15 business
  days) after deal notice
- LP remains in the fund if they opt out -- capital is held back for that
  deal only

---

### 2.6 Excuse Rights

**Mechanics:** LP is excused (not penalized) from a capital call for a
specific deal if the deal conflicts with their stated written investment policy.

**Distinction from opt-out:**
- Opt-out = discretionary LP decision (may or may not have a policy reason)
- Excuse = mandatory non-participation due to documented conflict with written
  investment policy

**Excused investments:**
- Excused LP is not penalized (no dilution, no fee penalty)
- Excused LP misses the investment return for that deal

**GP considerations:**
- Public pension plans often require excuse rights as a legal matter
- Foundation and endowment LPs with social screens may require excuse rights
- Consistent exercise of excuse rights may indicate the fund-LP fit is poor

## 3. Negotiation Sequencing Strategy

### 3.1 Anchor First: Set the MFN Floor Deliberately

**Why sequence anchors first:**
- The lowest fee rate granted to any non-excluded LP becomes the MFN floor
- All subsequent MFN-eligible LPs can ratchet to this floor
- If an anchor gets 1.20% and later a strategic LP negotiates 1.15%, the
  entire MFN-eligible pool ratchets to 1.15% -- the anchor indirectly caused
  cascades for 40 other LPs

**Best practice:**
1. Negotiate the anchor's economics first, before any mid-tier LP
2. Set the anchor's fee with explicit knowledge of what the MFN cascade would
   cost if any later LP gets a lower rate
3. Include the anchor in MFN exclusions if their commitment exceeds the
   exclusion threshold (common: >$50M or >15% of target)
4. The anchor exclusion must be in the fund documents (LPA or PPM), not just
   the side letter -- otherwise all LPs with MFN clauses can challenge it

---

### 3.2 Mid-Tier Fill: Sequential Standard LP Outreach

**After anchor close:**
1. Formal close announcement to pipeline LPs signals raise momentum
2. Priority order: strategic LPs with most leverage on your next raise (top
   institutional programs) before opportunistic standard LPs
3. For each strategic LP, have the scenario model run before entering
   negotiations -- know the MFN cascade cost before granting anything

**Tools:**
- Run `fund_fee_modeler.py --command scenario` before every material
  concession discussion
- Know your blended fee at every scenario outcome before sitting down

---

### 3.3 Final Close: Smaller and Later LPs at Standard Terms

**Final close dynamics:**
- LPs joining at final close have less leverage -- the fund is nearly full
- Use final close to fill up to hard cap with standard-term LPs
- Any fee holiday or early closer discount programs should have expired
- Final close LPs may still trigger MFN if they have MFN clauses -- watch
  the floor

**Final close GP discipline:**
- Do not open new fee negotiations at final close unless strategically critical
- An LP holding out for a better deal at final close can be told honestly that
  the fund is filling and the offer expires at the close date
- Scarcity of remaining capacity is genuine leverage

## 4. When to Hold vs When to Concede

### 4.1 Seller's Market (GP Has Leverage)

**Conditions:**
- Fund is oversubscribed (soft-circled > hard cap)
- Multiple close dates have hit targets early
- Placement agent reports LP demand exceeding supply
- Prior fund had top-quartile performance

**GP behavior in seller's market:**
- Hold on management fee -- standard terms for standard and strategic LPs
- Limited concessions only for true anchor LPs who bring raise-enabling size
- Co-invest fee-free concessions are acceptable (no MFN cascade risk)
- Reject fee holiday requests -- they compound over the fund life

**Key signal:** If the GP has to turn down LP capital to respect the hard cap,
every concession request should be declined.

---

### 4.2 Buyer's Market (LP Has Leverage)

**Conditions:**
- Raise is slower than projected
- Key LP targets have passed or gone silent
- Market context: rate rises, real estate market correction, LP rebalancing
- Peer managers are offering concessions to close LPs

**GP behavior in buyer's market:**
- Open fee concessions earlier in the negotiation with strategic LPs
- Offer 10-15 bps below standard to close verbal commits at first close
- Consider increasing org expense cap in lieu of deeper fee discounts
- Use co-invest and LPAC seats as primary value-adds before reducing fees

**Key signal:** If the fund misses its first close target by >25%, the GP is
in a buyer's market and should adjust negotiating posture.

---

### 4.3 First Fund (GP Has Least Leverage)

**Reality of first-time funds:**
- No audited track record; LPs are underwriting the team, not the history
- LPs will request deeper fee discounts as compensation for uncertainty
- GP commitment quality is heavily scrutinized -- fee waiver commitments often
  rejected by institutional LPs
- Seed investors / anchor LPs may request co-GP economics (promote sharing)

**GP strategy for first-time funds:**
- Limit fee discounts to 20 bps maximum even for anchors -- the fund cannot
  afford deep discounts without track record premium
- Offer non-fee concessions aggressively: LPAC, reporting, co-invest priority
- Get legal counsel on MFN drafting -- first funds make the most MFN mistakes
- Consider whether fund size target is achievable at current fee structure

## 5. Common GP Mistakes in Fee Negotiations

### Mistake 1: Setting the MFN Floor Too Low Too Early

**What happens:** GP closes an anchor at first close with 1.10% fee. Fund
documents have broad MFN (all LPs, blanket scope). By final close, 30 of 50
LPs have MFN clauses and all ratchet to 1.10%. Blended fee is 1.12% vs
planned 1.43%. Revenue loss: $990K/yr on a $300M fund.

**Prevention:**
- Model the full MFN cascade before agreeing to any anchor discount
- Use commitment-tier-matched MFN (anchor excluded from floor-setting)
- Include anchor exclusion in LPA, not just side letter

---

### Mistake 2: Fee Holidays That Compound

**What happens:** GP offers 6-month fee holiday to close a strategic LP at
first close. Later LP requests 12-month holiday. GP grants it. Third LP
requests 18 months citing MFN. Fee holiday is now MFN-eligible because
it was not excluded.

**Economic impact:** 18-month holiday on $100M of commitments at 1.50% =
$2.25M in foregone fees that cannot be recovered.

**Prevention:**
- Explicitly exclude fee holidays from MFN scope in the side letter
- Cap total fee holidays across all LPs at 6% of first-year fund revenue
- Never grant fee holidays that exceed investment period start date

---

### Mistake 3: Unlimited MFN Scope

**What happens:** MFN clause in side letter says "LP shall be entitled to any
more favorable terms granted to any other LP." No exclusions, no scope
limitations, no commitment-tier matching.

**Risk:** LP can claim MFN on any term -- co-invest allocations, org expense
caps, excuse rights, reporting frequency -- not just management fees.

**Prevention:**
- Define MFN scope explicitly: "fee-only MFN" or "commitment-tier-matched"
- Standard exclusions: GP affiliates, founding LPs, anchor LPs above threshold
- Include a catch-all exclusion: "terms granted in consideration of commitments
  materially larger than LP's commitment are excluded from MFN"

---

### Mistake 4: Fee Waiver GP Commitment

**What happens:** GP commits to fund via fee waiver (foregoes management fee)
rather than cash. Side letter grants fee waiver to anchor LP. Fee waiver now
exceeds GP's cash commitment. Institutional LPs realize the GP is not putting
real money at work.

**Market standard:** GP commitment should be minimum 1% of fund in cash. Fee
waiver may supplement but should not replace cash commitment for institutional
LPs.

**Prevention:**
- Structure GP commitment as cash minimum 1% + optional fee waiver supplement
- If GP cannot fund 1% cash, consider smaller fund target or co-GP arrangement
- Disclose GP commitment composition clearly in PPM

---

### Mistake 5: Late-Raise Fee Reductions

**What happens:** At final close, GP grants a 15 bps discount to one remaining
LP to hit the hard cap. MFN provisions are retroactive. All 45 previously
signed LPs ratchet to the new floor. Annual revenue loss: $450K on a $300M fund.

**Prevention:**
- Never grant fee reductions at final close unless the capital is essential
  to cross a meaningful milestone (e.g., minimum fund size)
- Model the retroactive MFN impact before agreeing to any final-close discount
- Use non-fee concessions exclusively at final close: co-invest, reporting

## 6. Counter-Offer Templates

### 6.1 Fee Reduction Request from Strategic LP

**LP request:** "We'd like to reduce the management fee from 1.50% to 1.25%
for our $25M commitment."

**GP counter-offer:**
> "Rather than reducing the management fee to 1.25%, we can offer 1.35% on
> your $25M commitment plus fee-free co-invest allocation of 15% of your
> commitment ($3.75M) on deals where co-invest is available. The fee-free
> co-invest at 1.50% waived fee is worth approximately $56K/year in waived
> fees, which -- combined with the 15 bps management fee reduction -- delivers
> a total economic benefit of approximately $94K/year equivalent, slightly
> more than your proposed straight 1.25% rate on $25M ($62.5K/year savings).
> This structure also preserves our ability to close other LPs at competitive
> terms."

**Why this works:**
- The co-invest fee waiver does not cascade through MFN (it is not a fund
  management fee)
- Total economic value to LP is higher than pure fee reduction
- Positions the GP as collaborative, not defensive

---

### 6.2 Anchor LP Pushing Below 30 bps Discount

**LP request:** "We're prepared to anchor at $50M but we need 1.10% given
the fund size risk we're taking."

**GP counter-offer:**
> "We understand the anchor risk premium you're pricing in. We can go to
> 1.15% for your $50M commitment, which we'll structure as a tiered rate:
> 1.50% on the first $10M (matching standard terms for smaller LPs), 1.15%
> on the balance. This delivers an effective rate of 1.22% on total commitment
> while maintaining our ability to hold standard terms for the broader LP base
> without triggering MFN cascade. We can supplement with LPAC seat, quarterly
> property-level reporting, and first-look co-invest at zero fee. We cannot
> go below 1.15% as it would create a cascade that costs the fund $2.1M/year
> given our current MFN-eligible pipeline."

**Why this works:**
- Tiered rate structure creates a blended effective rate acceptable to LP
  without setting a simple MFN floor of 1.10%
- Quantifies the MFN cascade cost to demonstrate GP constraint is real
- Packages non-fee concessions with meaningful value

---

### 6.3 Fee Holiday Request

**LP request:** "We typically require a 12-month fee holiday for first closes
to compensate for J-curve drag."

**GP counter-offer:**
> "A 12-month fee holiday across the investment period would cost the fund
> approximately $450K on your $30M commitment at 1.50%. We can offer a
> 6-month holiday beginning at your capital call (not at final close), which
> is worth $225K and aligns the concession with the period you have capital
> working in the ground. We'll also include this as a fee-only concession
> excluded from MFN scope, meaning it won't be available to other LPs at
> different commitment sizes. Alternatively, we can offer a 15 bps discount
> from your first close through investment period end -- same economic value,
> cleaner for our reporting."

---

### 6.4 MFN Clause Request from Mid-Tier LP

**LP request:** "Our investment policy requires an MFN clause in our side
letter."

**GP counter-offer:**
> "We can accommodate a commitment-tier-matched MFN -- meaning you are
> entitled to any more favorable management fee terms granted to other LPs
> with commitments within the same size tier as yours ($10M-$30M range).
> This does not extend to anchor LP terms (commitments above $50M), which are
> separately negotiated based on the risk they take in anchoring the raise.
> The MFN scope is limited to management fee only -- non-fee terms such as
> co-invest allocations, reporting packages, and advisory committee seats are
> excluded. This is consistent with how we've structured MFN for all LPs at
> your commitment level."
