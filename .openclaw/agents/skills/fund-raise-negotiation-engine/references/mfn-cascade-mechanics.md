# MFN Cascade Mechanics
# Most Favored Nation Provisions -- How They Work, How They Cascade, How to Limit Them

> **Source:** Fund formation practice norms. Consult fund counsel before
> drafting MFN provisions. State law and fund jurisdiction affect enforceability.

---

## 1. What MFN Actually Means in Fund Documents

### 1.1 Plain-Language Definition

A Most Favored Nation (MFN) clause in a limited partnership side letter gives
the LP the right to receive any more favorable terms that the GP grants to
any other LP in the same fund.

**Standard side letter language (broad MFN):**
> "To the extent the General Partner grants to any other Limited Partner in
> the Fund any terms, rights, or provisions that are more favorable than those
> set forth herein, the General Partner shall promptly notify the Limited
> Partner thereof and the Limited Partner shall be entitled, upon notice to
> the General Partner, to receive such more favorable terms."

**What "more favorable" means in practice:**
- Lower management fee rate
- Reduced carry
- Higher preferred return
- Longer fee holiday
- Larger org expense cap allocation
- Better co-invest economics (depending on scope)
- Any other term where the LP receiving it is economically better off

### 1.2 Where MFN Lives in Fund Documents

MFN provisions appear in three places, in order of legal precedence:

1. **Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA):** If MFN is baked into the LPA, it
   applies to all LPs unless explicitly excluded. Rarely done this way.
2. **Side Letter:** The most common location. LP-specific side letter grants
   MFN to that specific LP only.
3. **Subscription Agreement Representations:** Some SAs include MFN language
   by reference to side letter terms.

**GP control point:** MFN terms in the side letter are negotiated LP-by-LP.
The GP can offer different MFN scopes to different LPs, as long as the LPA
does not mandate uniform MFN treatment.

## 2. Types of MFN Scope

### 2.1 Blanket (All Terms) MFN

**Coverage:** Any term granted to any LP in the fund.

**Risk to GP:** Maximum. One concession to any LP on any term cascades to
all blanket MFN holders.

**Who requests it:** Sophisticated institutional LPs (pension funds, sovereign
wealth funds) with internal compliance teams that mandate it.

**GP negotiation response:**
- Never grant blanket MFN without exclusions
- At minimum, carve out: (a) anchor LP terms, (b) GP affiliate terms,
  (c) founding/seed LP terms, (d) terms in consideration of materially larger
  commitments, (e) non-fee terms

---

### 2.2 Fee-Only MFN

**Coverage:** Management fee and carry only. Non-fee terms (co-invest
allocations, reporting, LPAC seats, opt-outs, excuse rights) are excluded.

**Why GPs prefer this:**
- Non-fee concessions don't cascade -- the GP can offer co-invest to one LP
  without giving it to all MFN-eligible LPs
- Limits the blast radius of any single concession
- Easier to administer and track

**Standard exclusions still apply within fee-only scope:**
- Anchor LP fees excluded if commitment > threshold
- GP affiliates excluded
- Early closer discounts excluded (treated as a separate close-specific term)

---

### 2.3 Commitment-Tier-Matched MFN

**Coverage:** LP can only invoke MFN against other LPs with similar-sized
commitments (within the same commitment tier).

**How tiers are defined (typical structure):**
- Tier 1 (Emerging): <$5M
- Tier 2 (Standard): $5M-$25M
- Tier 3 (Strategic): $25M-$75M
- Tier 4 (Anchor): $75M+

An LP in Tier 2 cannot invoke MFN against an Anchor LP in Tier 4, even if
the anchor received a deeper fee discount.

**Why this is the strongest GP protection:**
- Anchor economics do not cascade to smaller LPs
- Tier structure is defensible because commitment size is an objective,
  observable criterion
- LPs at lower tiers cannot complain they "deserve" anchor terms they did not
  earn through commitment size

---

### 2.4 Custom / Hybrid MFN

**Coverage:** Negotiated scope that mixes elements of the above. Common
combinations:
- Fee-only, commitment-tier-matched (most protective for GP)
- Blanket, with specific exclusions for listed items (LP-friendly but with
  carve-outs negotiated item by item)
- Fee-only, with co-invest included but cap on co-invest allocation
  (preserves some protection while expanding scope)

## 3. Standard MFN Exclusions

The following exclusions are market standard and should appear in every MFN
side letter provision:

### 3.1 GP Affiliates

Employees, principals, and related entities of the GP often invest in the
fund at fee-free or near-fee-free terms. These terms cannot cascade to
third-party LPs.

**Standard language:**
> "For the avoidance of doubt, terms granted to affiliates of the General
> Partner, employees of the General Partner, or entities controlled by
> the General Partner are excluded from the scope of this MFN provision."

---

### 3.2 Founding / Seed LPs

LPs who provide seed capital before the fund reaches a defined minimum size
(e.g., $50M) often receive better terms as compensation for their early-stage
risk. These terms should be explicitly excluded from MFN scope.

**Standard language:**
> "Terms granted to Limited Partners who made commitments prior to the Fund
> reaching a first close of [$X] million are excluded from this MFN
> provision."

---

### 3.3 Anchor LPs Above a Threshold

**Standard language:**
> "Terms granted to Limited Partners whose aggregate commitments to the Fund
> exceed [$X] million [or X% of the Fund's Target Raise] are excluded from
> this MFN provision."

**Threshold setting:**
- Set the threshold above the LP's own commitment (otherwise the exclusion
  only protects anchors, not the full cascade)
- On a $300M fund, $50M+ exclusion (17% of target) keeps most LPs from
  accessing anchor economics

---

### 3.4 Strategic Investors in Non-Standard Capacities

**Covers:** Government agencies, development finance institutions, public
pension plans with statutory investment requirements, or LPs that provide
non-monetary value (e.g., property pipeline access, co-GP services).

**Language:**
> "Terms granted to Limited Partners in consideration of non-fee services,
> property access rights, co-GP arrangements, or government-mandated investment
> programs are excluded from this MFN provision."

---

### 3.5 Terms Requiring Materially Larger Commitments

**The most flexible catchall:**
> "Terms granted in consideration of commitments materially larger than the
> LP's commitment are excluded from this MFN provision."

**What "materially larger" means:**
- Not defined in the side letter (deliberately vague)
- Typically interpreted as 2x-3x the MFN-eligible LP's commitment
- If challenged, GP argues the economic rationale for the concession is the
  scale of commitment, not favoritism

## 4. Cascade Math: Worked Example with 10 LPs

### 4.1 Setup

**Fund:** Meridian Income Real Estate Fund V
**Target Raise:** $300M | **Hard Cap:** $350M
**Standard Management Fee:** 1.50% on committed capital
**MFN Provision:** Fee-only, commitment-tier-matched (Tier 3+ excluded for
  Anchor LPs above $50M)

| LP | Commitment | Tier | MFN Eligible | Negotiated Fee | Rate |
|----|-----------|------|--------------|----------------|------|
| LP 1 (CalPERS) | $75M | Anchor | No (>$50M exclusion) | Side letter | 1.20% |
| LP 2 (OTPP) | $50M | Anchor | No (>$50M exclusion) | Side letter | 1.25% |
| LP 3 (NC Pension) | $30M | Strategic | Yes | Side letter | 1.35% |
| LP 4 (Endowment A) | $25M | Strategic | Yes | Side letter | 1.40% |
| LP 5 (Insurance Co) | $20M | Strategic | Yes | None yet | 1.50% (standard) |
| LP 6 (FoF A) | $15M | Standard | Yes | None yet | 1.50% (standard) |
| LP 7 (FoF B) | $12M | Standard | Yes | None yet | 1.50% (standard) |
| LP 8 (Family Office) | $10M | Standard | Yes | Requesting 1.10% | -- |
| LP 9 (HNWI) | $8M | Standard | Yes | None yet | 1.50% (standard) |
| LP 10 (Foundation) | $5M | Standard | Yes | None yet | 1.50% (standard) |

**Total committed (LP 1-7 + LP 9-10):** $240M (before LP 8 decision)
**LP 8 requested fee:** 1.10%

---

### 4.2 Step 1: Identify Current MFN Floor

Before LP 8 negotiates:
- LP 3 has the lowest rate among MFN-eligible LPs: 1.35%
- LP 3 is in Tier 3 (Strategic). Under commitment-tier-matched MFN, LP 3
  only sets the floor for other Tier 3 LPs.
- Tier 2 (Standard) LPs (LP 5-LP 10) are compared against each other.
- Current MFN floor for Standard tier: 1.50% (no Standard LP has negotiated
  a discount yet)

---

### 4.3 Step 2: LP 8 Requests 1.10% Fee

If GP grants LP 8 a 1.10% fee (40 bps below standard), LP 8 becomes the new
MFN floor for all Standard-tier (Tier 2) MFN-eligible LPs.

**MFN cascade calculation:**

| LP | Commitment | Current Rate | Post-Cascade Rate | Annual Impact |
|----|-----------|--------------|-------------------|---------------|
| LP 5 (Insurance) | $20M | 1.50% | 1.10% | -$80,000/yr |
| LP 6 (FoF A) | $15M | 1.50% | 1.10% | -$60,000/yr |
| LP 7 (FoF B) | $12M | 1.50% | 1.10% | -$48,000/yr |
| LP 8 (Family Office) | $10M | 1.50% | 1.10% | -$40,000/yr |
| LP 9 (HNWI) | $8M | 1.50% | 1.10% | -$32,000/yr |
| LP 10 (Foundation) | $5M | 1.50% | 1.10% | -$20,000/yr |
| **TOTAL CASCADE** | **$70M** | **1.50%** | **1.10%** | **-$280,000/yr** |

**Direct cost of granting LP 8 its request:**
- LP 8 alone: $10M * (1.50% - 1.10%) = $40,000/yr
- But cascade adds $240,000/yr (6x the direct cost)
- Total annual cost: $280,000/yr
- Over a 10-year fund life: $2.8M in foregone fees

---

### 4.4 Step 3: Cascade-to-Direct Cost Ratio

**Cascade multiple:** $280,000 / $40,000 = 7.0x

This is precisely the scenario that should trigger the MFN cascade red flag:
cascade cost is >20% of direct concession value (it is 600% higher).

**GP recommendation:**
- Do not grant 1.10% to LP 8 at standard terms
- Counter with 1.35% (matching the current Strategic-tier MFN floor of LP 3,
  but LP 8 is not in Strategic tier, so this is a counter offer, not a match)
- Or: offer 1.40% with fee-free co-invest on their $10M commitment (co-invest
  waiver does not cascade through MFN)

---

### 4.5 Step 4: What the Dashboard Shows After Cascade

**If GP grants 1.10% to LP 8 and cascade occurs:**

| Metric | Before LP 8 | After Cascade |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Standard fee rate | 1.50% | 1.50% |
| Blended fee (all committed) | 1.39% | 1.28% |
| Annual fee revenue (on $240M) | $3,336,000 | $3,056,000 |
| Annual fee loss | -- | -$280,000 |
| Revenue loss over fund life | -- | -$2,800,000 |

## 5. Strategies for Limiting MFN Exposure

### 5.1 Commitment-Tier Matching

**Already covered in Section 2.3.** The single most effective structural
protection. Enforce in every MFN side letter:
- Define tiers explicitly in the side letter with dollar thresholds
- Tie the tiers to the fund's actual LP distribution (don't create a tier
  where only one LP sits)
- Include a tier-change provision: if LP increases commitment to a higher
  tier, MFN scope expands accordingly

---

### 5.2 Anchor Exclusion Floor

Set the anchor exclusion threshold above the highest non-anchor commitment
in the fund. If the largest non-anchor LP is at $30M, set the anchor
exclusion at $35M.

**Why this matters:**
- If anchor threshold is $50M and a strategic LP at $40M negotiates a deeply
  discounted rate, that rate cascades to all Standard-tier MFN holders even
  though the $40M LP is economically similar to an anchor
- Setting the threshold lower closes this gap

---

### 5.3 Fee-Only MFN

Keep non-fee concessions out of MFN scope entirely. This allows the GP to:
- Offer co-invest allocations, LPAC seats, enhanced reporting to select LPs
  without triggering MFN
- Reserve MFN cascade only for actual fee rate changes
- Use co-invest and non-fee concessions as negotiation tools with no cascade risk

---

### 5.4 Sunset Provisions

**Mechanics:** MFN clause applies only at the time of the LP's signing
(static snapshot), not retroactively to future side letters.

**Standard language:**
> "This MFN provision applies only to side letter terms entered into
> concurrently with or prior to the date of this Agreement. Terms granted
> after the date hereof are not subject to this MFN provision."

**Practical effect:** If LP 3 signs at first close with MFN, and LP 8 signs
at final close with a lower rate, LP 3's MFN is not triggered because LP 3's
MFN is limited to terms negotiated at the time of LP 3's signing.

**LP pushback:** Institutional LPs will resist this -- they want ongoing MFN
protection, not just a snapshot. Expect negotiation.

---

### 5.5 Dollar Cap on MFN Ratchet Benefit

**Mechanics:** MFN clause entitles LP to no more than X bps improvement from
their current rate.

**Standard language:**
> "Notwithstanding the foregoing, any adjustment to the management fee
> pursuant to this MFN provision shall not exceed [15] basis points below the
> management fee rate set forth in this Side Letter."

**Effect:** Even if another LP gets a very deep discount, the MFN-eligible LP
can only ratchet down by the capped amount.

---

## 6. Side Letter Provisions That Interact with MFN

### 6.1 Co-Invest Allocations

**Interaction:** If MFN scope includes co-invest terms, a co-invest fee waiver
granted to one LP cascades to all MFN-eligible LPs.

**Management:** Explicitly exclude co-invest terms from MFN scope:
> "For the avoidance of doubt, co-investment allocation rights, fees payable
> on co-investments, and carry on co-investments are not subject to this MFN
> provision."

---

### 6.2 Fee Offsets

**Background:** Fee offsets reduce management fees by a percentage of deal
fees (acquisition fees, disposition fees, financing fees) collected by the GP
from fund assets. Market standard: 50%-100% offset.

**MFN interaction:**
- If one LP's side letter provides for 100% fee offset and the standard is
  50%, MFN-eligible LPs with broader MFN scope may claim the higher offset
- Fee offsets change the effective fee rate LP pays, so fee-only MFN should
  include offset percentage as part of the "fee" definition

**Management:** Define fee offset as part of fee terms and include it in
commitment-tier-matched MFN scope (so it cascades only within tier):
> "For purposes of this MFN provision, 'management fee terms' includes the
> fee offset percentage set forth in this Side Letter."

---

### 6.3 Org Expense Caps

**Background:** GP charges organizational expenses to the fund up to a cap
(e.g., $500K). Some LPs negotiate a cap on their pro-rata share of org
expenses.

**MFN interaction:**
- An LP's side letter that reduces their org expense allocation triggers MFN
  if org expenses are within MFN scope
- The economic value is small per LP but cascades to large LP groups

**Management:** Explicitly exclude org expense caps from MFN scope, OR include
them in MFN only if the overall cap change is above a dollar threshold:
> "Adjustments to the organizational expense cap of less than $50,000 in
> aggregate impact are excluded from this MFN provision."

---

### 6.4 Redemption / Early Exit Rights

**Rarely granted but high MFN risk if cascaded:**
- A fund that allows one LP to exit early (return of capital) must grant
  this to all LPs if they have blanket MFN
- Most GPs explicitly exclude liquidity provisions from MFN scope

---

### 6.5 LPAC / Advisory Committee Governance Rights

**MFN interaction:** If one LP receives an LPAC voting seat with specific
governance rights (e.g., ability to consent to related-party transactions),
a blanket MFN could require the same rights for all MFN-eligible LPs.

**Practical problem:** LPAC governance rights typically require a limited
number of committee members to function. If 30 LPs all have voting rights,
the committee is unworkable.

**Management:** Explicitly exclude LPAC and governance rights from MFN scope:
> "Advisory committee seats, limited partner advisory committee membership,
> and governance rights are not subject to this MFN provision."
