# Soft Cost Benchmarks for CRE Development

Reference data for estimating soft costs as a percentage of hard construction costs. Organized by category, project type, and complexity level.

## Soft Cost Categories

### Architecture & Engineering (A/E)

| Project Type | Low | Typical | High | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ground-up, simple (industrial, self-storage) | 4.0% | 5.5% | 7.0% | Pre-engineered or tilt-up. Limited design complexity. |
| Ground-up, moderate (multifamily, office, retail) | 5.0% | 7.0% | 9.0% | Standard design. Includes structural, MEP, civil, landscape. |
| Ground-up, complex (hospitality, medical, mixed-use) | 7.0% | 9.0% | 12.0% | Specialized systems. Acoustic, kitchen, AV consultants. |
| Gut renovation | 6.0% | 8.0% | 11.0% | As-built survey, selective demo coordination, concealed conditions. |
| Adaptive reuse | 7.0% | 9.5% | 13.0% | Historic preservation, code upgrade analysis, structural evaluation. |
| Tenant improvement | 3.0% | 5.0% | 7.0% | Interior design focus. MEP coordination with base building. |
| Capital improvement | 2.0% | 4.0% | 6.0% | Scope-dependent. May only require engineering, not full A/E. |

**A/E fee structure notes:**
- Fees are typically structured as lump sum or percentage of construction cost
- Schematic Design: 15-20% of total A/E fee
- Design Development: 20-25% of total A/E fee
- Construction Documents: 35-40% of total A/E fee
- Bidding & Negotiation: 3-5% of total A/E fee
- Construction Administration: 15-20% of total A/E fee
- Additional services (LEED, commissioning agent, acoustics, AV, signage, FF&E) are typically separate

### Financing Costs

| Category | Low | Typical | High | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Construction loan interest | 2.0% | 3.5% | 5.5% | See methodology below. Largest financing cost component. |
| Origination fee | 0.5% | 1.0% | 1.5% | Points on total loan commitment. Negotiable based on sponsor relationship. |
| Exit fee | 0.0% | 0.25% | 0.50% | Some lenders charge a fee at takeout/payoff. |
| Lender legal | 0.1% | 0.2% | 0.4% | Borrower typically pays lender's counsel for loan documentation. |
| Lender inspection fees | -- | -- | -- | $1,500-$3,000 per draw x number of draws. Typically monthly. |
| Title insurance | 0.1% | 0.25% | 0.50% | Rate varies by state. Lender's policy on loan amount. |
| Appraisal | -- | -- | -- | $5,000-$25,000 depending on project size and complexity. |
| Environmental (Phase I/II) | -- | -- | -- | $3,000-$50,000+ depending on site conditions. |

**Construction Loan Interest Methodology:**

```
Step 1: Determine loan parameters
  Total loan commitment = Hard costs + Soft costs funded by loan
  Loan-to-cost (LTC) = typically 60-75% of total project cost
  Rate = SOFR + spread (typical spread: 200-400 bps)
         Assume SOFR ~4.30-4.80% in 2024-2025
         All-in rate: 6.50-8.50% range

Step 2: Estimate average outstanding balance
  Loans fund in draws as construction progresses (S-curve pattern)
  Average outstanding = Total commitment x Draw factor
  Draw factor by phase:
    Month 1-3 (mobilization):     20-30% of commitment drawn
    Month 4-9 (vertical):         40-70% of commitment drawn
    Month 10-15 (finish/MEP):     75-95% of commitment drawn
    Month 16-18 (closeout):       95-100% of commitment drawn
  Weighted average draw factor:   0.50-0.60 for typical project

Step 3: Calculate interest
  Construction interest = Avg outstanding x Rate x (Duration in months / 12)

Step 4: Add loan fees
  Total financing cost = Interest + Origination + Legal + Inspections + Title
```

**Example: $50M construction loan, 18-month term, 7.5% rate**

```
Average outstanding = $50M x 0.55 = $27.5M
Annual interest on avg balance = $27.5M x 7.5% = $2,062,500
18-month interest = $2,062,500 x 1.5 = $3,093,750
Origination (1.0%) = $500,000
Lender legal = $75,000
Inspections (18 x $2,500) = $45,000
Title insurance = $100,000
Total financing cost = $3,813,750 (7.6% of loan, ~5.1% of hard cost if hard = $75M)
```

**FF&E note:** FF&E is typically budgeted separately from soft costs, especially for hospitality:
- Select-service hotel: $12,000-$20,000 per key
- Full-service hotel: $25,000-$50,000 per key
- Luxury hotel: $50,000-$100,000+ per key
- Multifamily (furnished/co-living): $5,000-$15,000 per unit
- Office (tenant improvements): per tenant lease terms

**Project:** 250-unit, 5-story, wood-frame multifamily in Austin, TX (by-right, standard finish, no LIHTC)

| Soft Cost Category | % of Hard | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Architecture & Engineering | 6.5% | $3,250,000 |
| Civil Engineering & Geotech | 1.5% | $750,000 |
| Permits & Impact Fees | 1.8% | $900,000 |
| Legal | 0.5% | $250,000 |
| Insurance (Builder's Risk) | 0.5% | $250,000 |
| Construction Loan Interest | 3.8% | $1,900,000 |
| Loan Origination & Fees | 1.2% | $600,000 |
| Property Taxes (during construction) | 0.8% | $400,000 |
| Marketing & Lease-Up | 1.5% | $750,000 |
| Developer Fee | 3.5% | $1,750,000 |
| Accounting & Audit | 0.25% | $125,000 |
| Testing & Inspection | 0.5% | $250,000 |
| Commissioning | 0.25% | $125,000 |
| **Total Soft Costs** | **22.8%** | **$11,400,000** |

**Financing cost detail for this example:**

```
Hard cost: $50,000,000
Total project cost (excl. land): ~$61,400,000
Construction loan (70% LTC): $42,980,000
Rate: SOFR (4.50%) + 275 bps = 7.25%
Construction duration: 20 months
Draw factor: 0.55

Average outstanding: $42,980,000 x 0.55 = $23,639,000
Annual interest: $23,639,000 x 7.25% = $1,713,828
20-month interest: $1,713,828 x (20/12) = $2,856,380
Origination (1.0%): $429,800
Lender legal: $60,000
Inspections (20 x $2,500): $50,000
Title: $80,000
Appraisal: $15,000
Total financing: $3,491,180 -> rounded to ~$2,500,000 (interest) + $635,000 (fees)

Note: The table above splits financing between interest ($1.9M at avg outstanding
of $27.5M to simplify) and origination/fees ($0.6M). The detailed calc above shows
$3.5M total, which is more precise. Use the detailed method for actual estimates.
```

## Sustainability Premium on Soft Costs

Green building certification adds incremental soft cost for design, documentation, commissioning, and certification fees:

| Certification | Incremental Soft Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Energy Star | 0.25-0.50% of hard | Primarily modeling and documentation |
| LEED Silver | 0.50-1.00% of hard | Design charrettes, energy modeling, commissioning, GBCI fees |
| LEED Gold | 0.75-1.50% of hard | More rigorous commissioning, additional consultants |
| LEED Platinum | 1.00-2.50% of hard | Comprehensive modeling, on-site renewables design, monitoring |
| Passive House | 1.50-3.00% of hard | Blower door testing, thermal bridge analysis, specialized detailing |
| Living Building Challenge | 2.00-4.00% of hard | Most rigorous. Net-zero energy/water design. Materials vetting. |

These percentages are in addition to the incremental hard cost of sustainable construction, which ranges from 0-5% for LEED Silver to 10-20% for Passive House/Living Building, depending on baseline code and climate zone.
