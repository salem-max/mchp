# Construction Contract Analysis Guide

## Contract Types Comparison

### Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP)

**Structure**: GC provides a ceiling price inclusive of cost of work, GC fee, general conditions, and contingency. Owner pays actual costs up to the GMP. Savings below GMP are shared per negotiated split.

**When to use:**
- Design is 60-80% complete (enough for reliable pricing)
- Owner wants cost certainty before construction starts
- Project is straightforward with well-defined scope
- Timeline is critical (construction can start before 100% CDs)

**Key provisions:**
| Provision | Market Standard | Negotiate For |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| GC fee | 4-6% of cost of work | Fee cap, not percentage (avoids GC benefiting from cost increases) |
| General conditions | 6-10% of hard cost | Lump sum, not percentage; detailed staffing plan |
| Contingency | 3-5% (GC-controlled) | Shared contingency or owner access to GC contingency details |
| Savings split | 50/50 or 75/25 (owner/GC) | 75/25 or 80/20 in owner's favor with performance incentive |
| Change order markup | 10-15% (sub) + 5-10% (GC) | Cap combined markup at 15%; no markup on owner-directed deductions |
| Allowances | Line items for undefined scope | Minimize; convert to lump sums as design finalizes |

**Risk allocation:**
```
Owner bears risk of: Design changes, unforeseen conditions (above contingency),
                     owner-caused delays, material escalation (if no escalation clause)

GC bears risk of:    Construction means/methods, subcontractor performance,
                     productivity, cost overruns within defined scope,
                     schedule (liquidated damages)
```

**Advantages:**
- Cost certainty (ceiling price)
- Transparent cost accounting (open book)
- Faster start (can begin with incomplete design)
- Shared savings incentivizes GC efficiency

**Disadvantages:**
- GMP often includes padding (contingency on top of contingency)
- Open book auditing required (additional owner cost)
- GC may front-load schedule of values
- Scope gaps between GMP and final CDs create change orders

### Cost-Plus with Fee

**Structure**: Owner pays all actual costs of work plus a fixed or percentage fee. No ceiling. Owner has complete cost transparency.

**When to use:**
- Design is early or evolving (schematic or design development)
- Fast-track construction (start before design is complete)
- Renovation with significant unknowns
- Owner has experienced project management team to monitor costs

**Key provisions:**
| Provision | Market Standard | Negotiate For |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| GC fee | 4-8% of cost of work | Fixed fee (not percentage) to avoid perverse incentive |
| General conditions | Actual cost, reimbursable | Lump sum cap on general conditions |
| Cost tracking | Monthly cost reports | Real-time cost tracking, online portal access |
| Audit rights | Annual or project-end | Continuous audit rights, withhold 5% pending audit |
| Subcontracting | GC selects subs | Owner approval of all subs, competitive bidding required for packages > $X |

**Advantages:**
- Maximum flexibility for design changes
- No GMP padding or markup on undefined scope
- Can start immediately with incomplete design
- Full cost transparency

**Disadvantages:**
- No cost ceiling (owner bears all overrun risk)
- Requires heavy owner-side project management
- GC has limited incentive to control costs (especially % fee)
- Total cost unknown until project completion

### Stipulated Sum (Lump Sum / Fixed Price)

**Structure**: GC provides a fixed price for complete scope of work. GC bears risk of cost overruns within defined scope. Changes only via formal change orders.

**When to use:**
- Design is 100% complete with fully coordinated CDs
- Scope is well-defined with minimal unknowns
- Project is competitively bid (3+ GC bids)
- Owner wants maximum cost certainty and minimal oversight
- Simple/repetitive construction (e.g., warehouse, standard retail)

**Key provisions:**
| Provision | Market Standard | Negotiate For |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| Contract sum | Fixed, all-inclusive | Detailed schedule of values for progress payment verification |
| Change orders | Required for any scope change | Clear definition of what constitutes a "change" vs. included |
| Allowances | Limited, defined in contract | Convert all allowances to lump sums before execution |
| Retainage | 10% until substantial completion | Reduce to 5% after 50% complete; release at substantial completion minus punchlist |
| Schedule | Fixed duration, liquidated damages | Mutual accountability (both LD and early completion bonus) |

**Advantages:**
- Maximum cost certainty
- Competitive bidding drives lower prices
- Minimal owner oversight required
- Clear risk transfer to GC

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires 100% complete design (long pre-construction)
- GC includes risk premium in price (higher base cost)
- Change orders are expensive (GC controls pricing leverage)
- Adversarial dynamic (GC incentivized to cut corners)
- No cost transparency (closed book)

### Comparison Summary

| Factor | GMP | Cost-Plus | Stipulated Sum |
|--------|-----|-----------|----------------|
| Cost certainty | High (ceiling) | Low (no ceiling) | Highest (fixed) |
| Flexibility | Moderate | High | Low |
| Design completeness needed | 60-80% | 0-50% | 100% |
| Cost transparency | High (open book) | Highest | None (closed book) |
| Owner oversight required | Moderate | High | Low |
| GC risk premium | Moderate | Low | Highest |
| Change order cost | Moderate | Low (at cost + fee) | High |
| Best for | Most commercial CRE | Complex renovations, fast-track | Simple, well-defined scope |

## GC Fee Reasonableness Analysis

### Fee Components

| Component | Typical Range | What It Covers |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| GC fee (profit + home office OH) | 4-6% of cost of work | Corporate overhead allocation, profit margin |
| General conditions | 6-10% of hard cost | On-site supervision, temporary facilities, safety, cleanup |
| Insurance | 1.5-3% of hard cost | GL, workers comp, builders risk, umbrella |
| Bonds | 1-2% of contract sum | Performance and payment bonds (if required) |
| Subcontractor default insurance (SDI) | 0.5-1.5% | Alternative to subcontractor bonding |

### GC Fee Benchmarks by Project Size

| Project Hard Cost | Expected GC Fee | Notes |
|-------------------|-----------------|-------|
| < $5M | 6-8% | Small project, less competitive |
| $5M - $20M | 5-7% | Standard range |
| $20M - $50M | 4-6% | Competitive, experienced GCs |
| $50M - $100M | 3.5-5% | Negotiating leverage, volume |
| > $100M | 3-4.5% | Institutional, highly competitive |

### General Conditions Audit

General conditions should be provided as a detailed line-item breakdown. Review each line against these benchmarks.

| General Conditions Item | Typical Monthly Cost | Notes |
|------------------------|---------------------|-------|
| Project superintendent | $12,000-20,000/month | Full-time, on-site |
| Project manager | $10,000-18,000/month | May be part-time on smaller projects |
| Assistant PM / Project engineer | $7,000-12,000/month | Only on larger projects |
| Site office / trailer | $1,500-3,500/month | Rental + utilities |
| Temporary power | $2,000-5,000/month | Can be significant for high-rise |
| Temporary water | $500-1,500/month | |
| Temporary heat/cooling | $1,000-5,000/month | Seasonal |
| Temporary protection | $1,000-4,000/month | Floor/elevator protection |
| Dumpsters/waste removal | $2,000-6,000/month | |
| Site safety/OSHA compliance | $1,000-3,000/month | Safety officer, equipment |
| Final cleaning | Lump sum $0.50-1.50/SF | End of project |
| Hoisting/crane | $10,000-40,000/month | Varies greatly by project |
| Permits | Actual cost, pass-through | Verify against fee schedule |
| As-built drawings | Lump sum $5,000-15,000 | |
| Commissioning coordination | Lump sum $5,000-20,000 | GC's coordination role |

**Red flags in general conditions:**
- Staffing levels exceeding project complexity
- Monthly costs that don't decline as project winds down
- Lump sums that should be unit-rate or actual cost
- Duplicate charges (items in both GC fee and general conditions)
- Excessive vehicle/equipment charges

## Change Order Management Framework

### Change Order Categories

| Category | Definition | Who Pays | Markup Allowed |
|----------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Owner-directed changes | Owner requests scope addition or modification | Owner | Yes (full markup) |
| Design errors/omissions | Architect/engineer mistake in CDs | A/E (via E&O) or Owner | Limited (depends on contract) |
| Unforeseen conditions | Concealed conditions not discoverable before construction | Owner (typically) | Yes (reduced markup) |
| Regulatory changes | New code requirements enacted after contract | Owner | Yes |
| Value engineering | Cost reduction proposal | Shared savings | Fee on net savings |
| GC means/methods | GC changes approach for efficiency | GC (no cost to owner) | N/A |

### Change Order Review Checklist

For every change order, verify:

1. **Necessity**: Is this change truly necessary? Could the original scope address the issue?
2. **Scope verification**: Does the proposed scope match the change description?
3. **Pricing validation**:
   - Compare unit costs to original bid/schedule of values
   - Verify labor hours against RSMeans or independent estimate
   - Confirm material costs with supplier quotes (request 2+)
   - Check equipment rental rates against published rates (AED Blue Book)
   - Verify subcontractor quotes are competitive (request backup)
4. **Markup compliance**: Confirm markup percentages match contract terms
5. **Schedule impact**: Assess time extension request against critical path
6. **Credit offsets**: Identify any deleted scope that offsets the change cost
7. **Contingency application**: Determine if GC contingency should apply first

### Change Order Markup Structure

| Tier | Subcontractor Markup | GC Markup on Sub Work | GC Markup on Self-Performed |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Contract standard | 10-15% OH&P | 5-10% on sub cost | 10-15% OH&P |
| Recommended cap | 10% OH&P | 5% on sub cost | 10% OH&P |
| Total combined | -- | 15-20% total | 10-15% total |

Negotiate: No markup on deductive changes. Credits should be at full value, not reduced by fee.

## Contingency Tracking Methodology

### Contingency Types

| Type | Who Controls | Typical Size | Purpose |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Design contingency | Architect/Owner | 5-15% (declines with design completion) | Scope not yet designed/detailed |
| Construction contingency | GC (in GMP) | 3-5% | Means/methods, minor unforeseen, productivity |
| Owner contingency | Owner | 3-5% | Owner-directed changes, unforeseen conditions, tenant improvements |

### Contingency Burn Rate Tracking

Track monthly to project completion:

```
Contingency Remaining % = (Original Contingency - Approved Draws) / Original Contingency

Expected Burn Rate:
  0-25% complete: 0-15% of contingency consumed
  25-50% complete: 15-40% of contingency consumed
  50-75% complete: 40-70% of contingency consumed
  75-100% complete: 70-90% of contingency consumed
  Closeout: 90-100% consumed
```

**Warning thresholds:**
| Project Completion | Contingency Consumed | Status |
|--------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Any stage | < Expected burn | Green -- on track |
| Any stage | Within 10% of expected | Yellow -- monitor closely |
| < 50% complete | > 50% consumed | Red -- immediate review |
| < 75% complete | > 80% consumed | Red -- risk of overrun |

### GC Contingency Audit

In GMP contracts, require:
- Monthly contingency usage report with line-item detail
- Pre-approval for any single contingency draw > $25,000
- Distinction between "true" unforeseen and GC scope gaps
- Unused contingency returns to owner (or shared per savings split)

## Payment Application Review Process

### AIA G702/G703 Review

Standard payment application format (AIA Document G702 -- Application and Certificate for Payment, G703 -- Continuation Sheet).

**Review steps:**

1. **Schedule of Values verification**: Compare line items to contract breakdown
   - No front-loading (early trades should not be disproportionately valued)
   - Material stored off-site: require bonded warehouse and insurance documentation
   - Retainage correctly calculated (typically 10% until 50% complete, then 5%)

2. **Percentage complete validation**:
   - Walk the site before approving -- visual verification
   - Compare claimed % to superintendent's daily reports
   - Check that stored materials have delivery tickets and invoices
   - Verify subcontractor work matches sub's invoice to GC

3. **Lien waiver collection**:
   - Conditional lien waiver from GC for current application
   - Unconditional lien waiver from GC for prior application
   - Conditional lien waivers from all subs/suppliers for current period
   - Unconditional lien waivers from all subs/suppliers for prior period
   - Track on lien waiver log -- never pay without complete waivers

4. **Retainage management**:
   | Milestone | Retainage Rate | Release Conditions |
   |-----------|---------------|-------------------|
   | 0-50% complete | 10% | Standard |
   | 50-100% complete | 5% (reduced) | GC requests, owner approves |
   | Substantial completion | Release to 5% of remaining | Punchlist items identified |
   | Final completion | Release remaining | All punchlist complete, final lien waivers |

5. **Compliance checks per application:**
   - [ ] Certified payroll current (if prevailing wage applies)
   - [ ] Insurance certificates current (all parties)
   - [ ] Permits current and posted
   - [ ] Schedule update submitted with application
   - [ ] Change orders incorporated in schedule of values
   - [ ] No outstanding notices of non-compliance

### Payment Timeline

| Event | Standard | Best Practice |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| GC submits application | 25th of month | Consistent monthly date |
| Architect/CM reviews | 5-7 business days | Avoid delays -- trigger interest clauses |
| Owner reviews | 3-5 business days | |
| Payment issued | Within 30 days of receipt | Prompt pay per contract and state law |

**Prompt payment statutes:** Most states require payment within 20-30 days of approved application. Interest accrues on late payments (typically 1-1.5%/month). Know your state's statute.

## Red Flags in GC Proposals

| Red Flag | What It Means | Action |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| Fee above 7% | Above market, or project too small for this GC | Negotiate or re-bid |
| General conditions > 12% | Overstaffed or padded | Request detailed breakdown and staffing plan |
| Excessive allowances | Undefined scope = future change orders | Push to convert allowances to lump sums |
| Vague exclusions list | GC will claim these as extras | Require explicit inclusion/exclusion matrix |
| No detailed schedule of values | Cannot verify progress payments | Require detailed SOV before contract execution |
| Contingency > 5% (in GMP) | Hidden padding | Negotiate contingency down or require shared access |
| Self-performance > 30% | Less competitive pricing | Require competitive sub bids for self-performed trades |
| Retainage release at 50% | Loses leverage for punchlist completion | Hold retainage until substantial completion minimum |
| No liquidated damages | No schedule accountability | Include mutual LD/bonus structure |
| Broad force majeure clause | GC avoids schedule responsibility | Narrow to true force majeure events, require mitigation |
