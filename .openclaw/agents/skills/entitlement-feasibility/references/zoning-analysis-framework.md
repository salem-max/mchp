## Approval Pathway Mapping

### Decision Tree

```
Is the proposed use PERMITTED in the current zoning?
  YES --> Does the project meet ALL dimensional requirements?
    YES --> AS-OF-RIGHT (administrative approval only)
    NO --> How significant are the dimensional gaps?
      MINOR (1-2 small variances) --> VARIANCE (ZBA)
      MODERATE (multiple variances or larger gaps) --> SITE PLAN REVIEW (Planning Board)
      MAJOR (fundamental dimensional non-compliance) --> PLANNED DEVELOPMENT (Planning Board + Council)
  NO --> Is the use listed as CONDITIONAL/SPECIAL?
    YES --> CONDITIONAL USE PERMIT (Planning Board/ZBA)
    NO --> Is the use consistent with the COMPREHENSIVE PLAN future land use?
      YES --> REZONING (Planning Board recommendation + City Council)
      NO --> COMPREHENSIVE PLAN AMENDMENT + REZONING (most difficult path)
```

### Approval Bodies and Roles

| Body | Role | Typical Meeting Frequency | Decision Type |
|------|------|--------------------------|---------------|
| Planning staff | Technical review, staff report | Continuous | Recommendation |
| Design review board | Architectural/aesthetic review | Monthly | Advisory or binding |
| Planning board/commission | Land use decisions, site plans | Monthly or bi-monthly | Quasi-judicial |
| Zoning board of adjustment (ZBA) | Variances, special exceptions | Monthly | Quasi-judicial |
| City council/commission | Rezonings, comprehensive plan | Bi-monthly or monthly | Legislative |
| Historic preservation | COA for historic districts | Monthly | Binding in district |
| Environmental review | Wetlands, stormwater, SEPA/NEPA | Varies | Regulatory |

### Approval Type Details

**As-of-Right (Administrative)**
- Triggers: Proposed use is permitted; all dimensional standards met
- Process: Building permit application, plan review, permit issuance
- Typical timeline: 2-4 months
- Estimated cost: $5,000-25,000 (permit fees, plan review)
- Risk level: Low (ministerial -- staff must approve if code is met)

**Site Plan Review (Planning Board)**
- Triggers: Permitted use requiring planning board review (often based on size threshold)
- Process: Pre-application meeting, application, staff review, public notice, public hearing, decision
- Typical timeline: 4-8 months
- Estimated cost: $15,000-50,000 (application fees, engineering, legal, design)
- Risk level: Low-Moderate (board has discretion on site design, not use)

**Variance (Zoning Board of Adjustment)**
- Triggers: Dimensional non-compliance (setback, height, parking, etc.)
- Legal standard (most jurisdictions): Applicant must demonstrate:
  1. Unnecessary hardship OR practical difficulty (varies by state)
  2. Unique property conditions (not self-created)
  3. Not contrary to public interest
  4. Minimum variance necessary to afford relief
  5. Variance will not alter essential character of neighborhood
- Typical timeline: 6-12 months
- Estimated cost: $20,000-75,000 (application, legal, engineering, expert witnesses)
- Risk level: Moderate (quasi-judicial, evidence-based)

**Conditional/Special Use Permit**
- Triggers: Use listed as conditional in zoning district
- Review criteria (typical):
  1. Consistent with comprehensive plan
  2. Compatible with adjacent uses
  3. Adequate infrastructure (roads, utilities, schools)
  4. Minimizes adverse impacts (traffic, noise, light)
  5. Meets any use-specific conditions in code
- Conditions commonly imposed: hours of operation, screening, traffic mitigation, phasing
- Typical timeline: 4-8 months
- Estimated cost: $25,000-100,000
- Risk level: Moderate

**Rezoning (City Council)**
- Triggers: Proposed use not permitted; seeking zoning district change
- Process: Pre-application, application, planning staff review, planning board public hearing and recommendation, city council public hearing and vote
- Review criteria: Consistency with comprehensive plan, impact on surrounding area, infrastructure adequacy, changed conditions since original zoning
- Typical timeline: 12-24 months
- Estimated cost: $50,000-250,000+ (legal, planning consultants, traffic studies, community engagement)
- Risk level: High (legislative -- council has broad discretion)

**Planned Development (PD/PUD)**
- Triggers: Large-scale or mixed-use project seeking flexible zoning in exchange for public benefits
- Process: Concept plan, preliminary plan, final plan (often 3-stage approval)
- Benefits: Flexibility on density, setbacks, use mix, parking
- Obligations: Public amenities, affordable housing, infrastructure, design standards
- Typical timeline: 18-36 months
- Estimated cost: $100,000-500,000+ (multi-disciplinary consultants, legal, community engagement)
- Risk level: High (complex, political, multi-stage)

## Political Risk Assessment

### Stakeholder Mapping

| Stakeholder | Influence Level | Likely Position | Engagement Strategy |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Elected officials (council/commission) | High | Varies | Early briefing, address concerns pre-hearing |
| Planning staff | High | Technical/neutral | Pre-application meetings, responsive to comments |
| Immediate neighbors | High | Often opposed | Direct outreach, address concerns, good-neighbor agreement |
| Neighborhood association | Medium-High | Varies | Present at meetings, community benefits |
| Local media | Medium | Neutral to negative | Proactive messaging, project narrative |
| Special interest groups | Medium | Depends on project | Engage early if environmental, historic, or housing concerns |
| General public | Low-Medium | Usually unengaged | Public notice compliance, accessible information |

### Political Risk Scoring

| Factor | Score 1 (Low Risk) | Score 3 (Moderate) | Score 5 (High Risk) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Elected official support | Publicly supportive | Neutral/unknown | Publicly opposed |
| Neighborhood sentiment | Supportive or neutral | Mixed | Organized opposition |
| Project consistency with comp plan | Fully consistent | Partially consistent | Inconsistent |
| Prior similar approvals | Precedent exists | No precedent | Prior denial of similar |
| Election cycle | Non-election year | Pre-election, not controversial | Election year, hot-button issue |
| Media attention | None | Some coverage | Active media campaign |
| Environmental/historic sensitivity | None | Minor concerns | Major protected resource |

Composite political risk score: Average of all factors.
- 1.0-2.0: Favorable political environment, proceed with standard engagement
- 2.0-3.0: Manageable risk, increase community engagement budget
- 3.0-4.0: Significant risk, consider design modifications or phasing
- 4.0-5.0: Very high risk, consider alternative sites or fundamental redesign

## Entitlement Value Quantification

### Residual Land Value Method

The entitlement process creates value by converting land from its current highest-and-best use to a more intensive use. Quantify this as the difference in residual land value.

```
Residual Land Value = Stabilized Value - Development Cost

Where:
  Stabilized Value = Stabilized NOI / Cap Rate
  Development Cost = Hard Costs + Soft Costs + Financing + Developer Fee + Contingency
```

### Risk-Adjusted Entitlement Value

```
Entitlement Value = (Post_Entitlement_Land_Value - Pre_Entitlement_Land_Value)
                    * Probability_of_Approval
                    * Time_Discount_Factor
                    - Entitlement_Costs

Time_Discount_Factor = 1 / (1 + discount_rate) ^ years_to_approval
```

**Worked Example:**
| Input | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Pre-entitlement land value (current zoning) | $2,000,000 |
| Post-entitlement land value (proposed zoning) | $5,500,000 |
| Gross entitlement value | $3,500,000 |
| Probability of approval (rezoning) | 65% |
| Expected time to approval | 18 months |
| Discount rate | 15% |
| Time discount factor | 1/(1.15)^1.5 = 0.812 |
| Entitlement costs (legal, consultants, fees) | $175,000 |
| **Risk-adjusted entitlement value** | **(3,500,000 * 0.65 * 0.812) - 175,000 = $1,671,300** |
| Maximum bid premium for entitled land over unentitled | $1,671,300 |

### Approval Probability Estimation

| Approval Type | Base Probability | Adjustments |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| As-of-right | 95-99% | Reduce for plan review bottleneck |
| Site plan review | 85-95% | Reduce for design concerns, neighbor opposition |

| Variance (major) | 50-70% | Reduce if significant community opposition |

## Pre-Application Diligence Checklist

- [ ] Obtain zoning verification letter from municipality
- [ ] Review complete zoning ordinance for applicable district
- [ ] Check all overlay districts (historic, environmental, airport, design)
- [ ] Review comprehensive plan / future land use map
- [ ] Search pending zoning changes and comprehensive plan amendments
- [ ] Review building code requirements (IBC edition adopted, local amendments)
- [ ] Identify impact fee schedule (transportation, parks, schools, water/sewer)
- [ ] Review inclusionary zoning / affordable housing requirements
- [ ] Research recent approval history for similar projects (last 3-5 years)
- [ ] Identify council district representative and political orientation
- [ ] Research neighborhood association contacts and meeting schedule
- [ ] Review environmental constraints (wetlands, floodplain, endangered species)
- [ ] Check utility capacity (water, sewer, electric, gas)
- [ ] Identify transportation/traffic study requirements
- [ ] Schedule pre-application meeting with planning staff
- [ ] Engage land use attorney for jurisdiction-specific guidance
