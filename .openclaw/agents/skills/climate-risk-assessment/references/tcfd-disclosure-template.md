# TCFD Disclosure Template for Commercial Real Estate

## Background

The Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) framework is the global standard for climate risk reporting. GRESB (Global Real Estate Sustainability Benchmark) has aligned its assessment with TCFD. Institutional LPs increasingly require TCFD-aligned disclosure. This template provides CRE-specific content for each TCFD pillar.

---

## Pillar 1: Governance

### Recommended Disclosures

**a) Board oversight of climate-related risks and opportunities**

Template language:

> [Company name]'s Board of Directors maintains oversight of climate-related risks through [choose applicable]:
>
> - A dedicated ESG/Sustainability Committee that meets [quarterly/semi-annually]
> - Integration of climate risk into the existing Risk Committee's mandate
> - Board-level review of climate risk assessments at least [frequency]
>
> The Board receives reporting on:
> - Portfolio-level climate risk scores and year-over-year trends
> - Capital expenditure plans for emissions reduction and climate adaptation
> - Regulatory compliance status (Building Performance Standards)
> - Insurance portfolio adequacy against physical climate risks
> - Progress toward stated emissions reduction targets

**b) Management's role in assessing and managing climate-related risks**

Key roles to define:

| Role | Climate Responsibility | Reporting Line |
|------|----------------------|---------------|
| Chief Sustainability Officer / VP Sustainability | Overall climate strategy, target setting, disclosure | CEO / Board |
| Head of Asset Management | Property-level decarbonization execution | COO / CSO |
| Head of Acquisitions | Climate risk screening in underwriting | CIO |
| Head of Risk Management | Physical risk assessment, insurance adequacy | CFO / CRO |
| Director of Engineering | Energy efficiency, BMS optimization, retrofits | VP Asset Mgmt |
| Head of Investor Relations | GRESB submission, LP climate reporting | CFO |

Process documentation:
- Annual climate risk review cadence
- Integration into investment committee memos
- Escalation triggers (e.g., new BPS legislation, catastrophic event, rating downgrade)

### Recommended Disclosures

**a) Climate-related risks and opportunities identified**

**Physical Risks (Acute and Chronic)**

| Risk Type | Hazard | Portfolio Exposure | Financial Impact Channel | Time Horizon |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|------------------------|-------------|
| Acute | Hurricane/typhoon | [X]% of AUM in hurricane-prone MSAs | Property damage, business interruption, insurance cost | Short-medium |

| Chronic | Extreme heat | [X] assets in CDD-increasing zones | Increased cooling cost, equipment stress, comfort complaints | Medium-long |
| Chronic | Water stress | [X] assets in high water-stress basins | Increased water cost, landscape restrictions, cooling tower limits | Medium-long |

**Transition Risks**

| Risk Type | Driver | Portfolio Exposure | Financial Impact Channel | Time Horizon |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|------------------------|-------------|
| Policy/Legal | Building Performance Standards | [X] assets in BPS jurisdictions | Compliance capex, penalties if non-compliant | Short-medium |

| Technology | Electrification costs | [X] assets with fossil fuel systems | Capex for heat pump conversion, stranded gas infrastructure | Medium |

**Opportunities**

| Opportunity | Description | Financial Benefit |
|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| Energy efficiency retrofit | Reduce operating costs, improve NOI | 10-30% utility cost reduction, 5-15% NOI improvement |
| Green certification premium | LEED, ENERGY STAR, WELL | 5-15% rent premium, 10-20% value premium |
| On-site renewable energy | Solar PV, battery storage | Reduced energy cost, resilience, REC revenue |
| Green financing | Green bonds, sustainability-linked loans | 5-25 bps interest rate reduction |
| Climate adaptation | Flood barriers, resilient design | Reduced insurance cost, preserved asset value |
| Regulatory compliance leadership | Early BPS compliance | Avoid penalties, attract ESG-conscious tenants |

**b) Impact on strategy, business, financial planning**

Document how climate considerations affect:

1. **Investment strategy**: Climate risk screening criteria for acquisitions (e.g., no assets in FEMA Zone V, maximum physical risk score)
2. **Capital allocation**: Annual capex budget for decarbonization and adaptation (target: [X]% of GAV)
3. **Hold period**: Adjusted hold/disposition strategy for high-climate-risk assets
4. **Asset management**: Property-level decarbonization roadmaps, green capex programs
5. **Leasing**: Green lease clauses, tenant engagement on energy/emissions
6. **Disposition**: Climate risk disclosure in marketing, buyer diligence expectations
7. **Financing**: Green bond framework, sustainability-linked loan covenants

**c) Resilience of strategy under climate scenarios**

Scenario analysis framework:

| Scenario | Global Warming | Description | Key Assumptions |
|----------|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Orderly Transition | 1.5C (SSP1-2.6) | Strong policy action, rapid decarbonization | Aggressive BPS, carbon price $100+/tCO2, full electrification by 2040 |
| Disorderly Transition | <2.0C (SSP1-2.6 delayed) | Late policy action, abrupt transition | Sudden carbon pricing, stranded fossil assets, retrofit bottleneck |
| Hot House World | >3.0C (SSP5-8.5) | Failed mitigation, severe physical impacts | Extreme weather, 3-6ft SLR by 2100, insurance retreat |

For each scenario, model:
- Portfolio valuation impact (% change in NAV)
- Operating cost trajectory (energy, insurance, water, maintenance)
- Capex requirements (compliance, adaptation)
- Revenue impact (tenant demand, rent growth, vacancy)
- Stranded asset identification (assets that become uneconomic)

## Pillar 3: Risk Management

### Recommended Disclosures

**a) Process for identifying and assessing climate-related risks**

Acquisition screening checklist:
1. Physical risk scoring (5 hazards per physical risk framework)
2. BPS jurisdiction compliance gap analysis
3. Energy audit / ENERGY STAR benchmarking review
4. Emissions intensity baseline and LL97/BPS penalty exposure
5. Insurance market assessment (availability, pricing trajectory)
6. Climate-adjusted cap rate analysis (risk premium for exposed assets)

Asset management ongoing assessment:
1. Annual ENERGY STAR benchmarking and emissions tracking
2. Quarterly insurance adequacy review
3. Annual physical risk score update (when new data releases)
4. BPS compliance tracking and penalty forecasting
5. Tenant green lease compliance monitoring

**b) Process for managing climate-related risks**

Risk management tools:

| Risk | Management Strategy | Responsible Party | Frequency |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Physical: flood | Insurance, adaptation investment, property-level plans | Risk Mgmt + Asset Mgmt | Annual |
| Physical: wind | Insurance, building hardening, IBHS Fortified | Risk Mgmt + Engineering | Annual |
| Physical: wildfire | Insurance, defensible space, WUI assessment | Risk Mgmt + Property Mgmt | Annual |
| Transition: BPS | Decarbonization roadmap, capex planning | Sustainability + Asset Mgmt | Quarterly |
| Transition: carbon price | Emissions tracking, reduction program | Sustainability | Quarterly |
| Transition: market | Green certification, tenant engagement | Leasing + Sustainability | Ongoing |
| Transition: technology | Electrification planning, equipment lifecycle | Engineering | Annual |

**c) Integration into overall risk management**

Climate risk integration points:
- Investment Committee: climate risk score required in all acquisition memos
- Annual business plan: property-level decarbonization budget included
- Quarterly portfolio review: climate KPI dashboard (emissions, energy, certifications)
- Insurance renewal: physical risk data informs program structure
- LP reporting: annual ESG/climate report aligned with TCFD

## Pillar 4: Metrics and Targets

### Recommended Disclosures

**a) Metrics used to assess climate-related risks and opportunities**

Core metrics (annual tracking):

| Metric | Unit | Scope | Reporting Standard |
|--------|------|-------|-------------------|
| Absolute GHG emissions (Scope 1+2) | tCO2e | Portfolio | GHG Protocol |
| GHG emissions intensity | kgCO2e/SF | Property + portfolio | GHG Protocol / GRESB |
| Energy consumption (site) | kWh, therms | Property + portfolio | ENERGY STAR |
| Energy use intensity (site EUI) | kBtu/SF/yr | Property + portfolio | ENERGY STAR |
| Energy use intensity (source EUI) | kBtu/SF/yr | Property + portfolio | ENERGY STAR |
| ENERGY STAR score | 1-100 | Property | EPA |
| Green-certified area | SF and % of portfolio | Portfolio | LEED/WELL/ES |
| Renewable energy (% of consumption) | % | Property + portfolio | GHG Protocol |
| Water consumption intensity | gal/SF | Property + portfolio | GRESB |
| Waste diversion rate | % | Property + portfolio | GRESB |
| Physical risk score | 1-5 composite | Property + portfolio | Internal methodology |
| BPS compliance status | Compliant/Non-compliant | Property | Jurisdiction-specific |
| Estimated BPS penalty exposure | $/yr | Property + portfolio | Jurisdiction-specific |
| Green capex (decarbonization + adaptation) | $ and % of GAV | Portfolio | Internal |

**b) Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions**

Reporting framework:

| Scope | Boundary | Data Source | Methodology |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Scope 1 | Landlord-controlled combustion | Utility bills, fuel receipts | GHG Protocol, EPA emission factors |
| Scope 2 (location) | Purchased electricity, steam | Utility bills | eGRID factors |
| Scope 2 (market) | Adjusted for RECs/PPAs | Utility bills + REC certificates | Residual mix factors |
| Scope 3 Cat 13 | Tenant energy (NNN) | Green lease data requests, estimates | Proxy methods if data unavailable |
| Scope 3 Cat 1 | Purchased goods (construction) | Spend data | EEIO factors |
| Scope 3 Cat 5 | Waste | Waste hauler reports | EPA WARM model |

Data quality scoring:
| Quality Level | Description | Application |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Measured | Actual meter/bill data for 12 months | Best -- use for all landlord-controlled |
| Estimated | Partial data extrapolated | Acceptable for tenant spaces without data |
| Proxy | Portfolio average or industry benchmark | Last resort for newly acquired |

**c) Targets and performance**

Target-setting framework:

| Target Type | Example | Alignment |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| Near-term (2025-2030) | 30% reduction in Scope 1+2 intensity from 2019 baseline | SBTi, Paris-aligned |
| Medium-term (2030-2040) | 50-65% reduction; 100% renewable electricity | SBTi net-zero standard |
| Long-term (2040-2050) | Net-zero Scope 1+2+3 | CRREM, Paris Agreement |
| Certification | 80% of portfolio ENERGY STAR certified by 2028 | GRESB best practice |
| Renewable energy | 50% renewable electricity by 2030, 100% by 2040 | RE100 |
| Physical risk | No new acquisitions with composite risk score > 4 | Internal risk appetite |

## Stranded Asset Probability Assessment

### Definition
A stranded asset is a property whose economic value is materially impaired due to climate-related factors before the end of its expected economic life.

### Stranding Pathways
| Pathway | Mechanism | Timeline | Indicator |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Regulatory | BPS penalties exceed NOI margin | 5-10 years | Penalty-to-NOI ratio > 15% |
| Physical | Chronic flooding makes property inaccessible | 10-30 years | >26 flood events/yr projected |
| Market | Tenants refuse non-certified space | 5-15 years | Rising vacancy, green flight |
| Financial | Insurance unavailable or unaffordable | 5-15 years | Premium > 3% of asset value |
| Technology | Fossil fuel systems become unleasable | 10-20 years | Gas ban legislation, tenant mandates |

### Stranding Probability Score
```
Stranding_Probability = weighted_average(
  0.30 * regulatory_risk_score,
  0.25 * physical_risk_score,
  0.20 * market_risk_score,
  0.15 * financial_risk_score,
  0.10 * technology_risk_score
)
```

| Score | Probability | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| 1.0-2.0 | <5% stranding in 15 years | Monitor |
| 2.0-3.0 | 5-15% | Develop mitigation plan |
| 3.0-4.0 | 15-35% | Active retrofit or disposition planning |
| 4.0-5.0 | >35% | Immediate disposition or transformative investment |

### CRREM (Carbon Risk Real Estate Monitor) Integration
CRREM provides science-based carbon pathways for CRE by property type and country. Assets exceeding the CRREM pathway are at risk of stranding.

| Property Type | 2025 Pathway (kgCO2e/m2) | 2030 Pathway | 2050 Pathway |
|---------------|--------------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Office | 45-55 | 30-40 | 0-5 |
| Retail | 50-65 | 35-45 | 0-5 |
| Residential | 25-35 | 15-25 | 0-5 |
| Hotel | 55-70 | 40-50 | 0-5 |
| Industrial | 20-30 | 15-20 | 0-5 |

Process:
1. Plot each asset's current emissions intensity on the CRREM pathway
2. Identify "stranding year" -- when asset crosses above the pathway
3. Quantify retrofit capex needed to remain below pathway
4. Compare capex to disposition proceeds (hold vs sell decision)
